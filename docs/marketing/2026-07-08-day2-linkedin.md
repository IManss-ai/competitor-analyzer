## DRAFT A — "the honest scoreboard" (recommended)

I launched Rivalscope on Product Hunt yesterday. Here is the honest scoreboard, 24 hours in.

I am not Featured. If you know Product Hunt, that is the section that actually gets you seen, and it is decided by moderators before you launch, not by how many upvotes you get. I did not line that up. So I started the day buried in the "all products" list where almost nobody looks.

What moved the needle was not the platform. It was people.

I messaged my nFactorial network one by one. Not a broadcast, just honest notes to people I actually share a room with. About 1 in 5 replied. A few upvoted. One told me my positioning was off and exactly how to fix it, which is worth more than the upvote. One person clocked that part of my outreach was automated and called me on it, fair enough, that one is on me.

We climbed from rank 67 to around 40 on the back of that. Not a rocket. Real people, one message at a time.

The lesson I am taking: the launch is not the moment the world discovers you. It is the day you find out who is actually in your corner, and how good your product has to be to earn the next conversation.

Rivalscope writes you a battle card on any competitor: how they position, the complaints buried in their G2 and Trustpilot reviews, and five plays to win the deal. Then it watches their site and tells you when they move.

Still early. Still reading every piece of feedback. If you have ever lost a deal because a competitor moved and you found out too late, I would like you to try it and tell me what is broken.

## DRAFT B — "one piece of feedback" (shorter, product-forward)

24 hours into launching Rivalscope, the most useful thing that happened was not an upvote.

Someone in my network replied, tried it, and told me my positioning was wrong. Specifically wrong. The kind of feedback you cannot buy and usually cannot get people to give you.

That is the whole reason I launched before it was polished. Not to win a badge. To get people who compete for real customers to point at the thing and say "this part does not work yet."

Here is what it does when it does work: you paste a competitor's URL, and about a minute later you get a battle card. How they position. The complaints that keep repeating in their G2 and Trustpilot reviews. Five plays to win deals against them. Then it keeps watching their site and pings you when pricing or messaging changes.

If you sell against the same few names every quarter, point it at one of them and tell me what it gets wrong.
